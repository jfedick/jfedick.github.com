Things I've Learned in Ruby on Rails #1

You can override models in migrations.

Today, I was working with a default_scope that would aggregate rows of data based on a particular field (this field was added using a migration before the default_scope had been implemented). This worked fine until someone else had to grab my code. When they tried to run the migration (to add the field), it failed because the field didn’t exist yet (even though the migration was originally run to create that field). It’s sort of a chicken-and-egg problem. To fix it, I needed to override the classes that were accessed in the migrations to remove the default scope. I reloaded the database and found that, if you migrated from scratch, there were several migrations that needed the class overloading. Once this was fixed everything worked fine. I just need to remember to override a migration whenever I create a new one that does not use the default_scope.

A TEXT POST

Site idea

The past couple days, I’ve been creating a character to play in my buddy Ron’s RPG on RPOL.net. They have a pretty terrible character creation system, which is barely better than just sitting down and writing everything out on paper. I know I can come up with a site that does this stuff better. Especially character creation- that’s my idea. I could streamline character creation so that anyone can do it and you don’t have to read a ton of text to figure everything out.

There would be a progress bar that would tell you how far you’ve progressed and how far until completion. Each step would have you fill out a certain part of your character. Some parts you could skip and come back to and some would need to be completed in order. This would allow flexibility for things you need to come back to.

So it would look something like this. You start out by choosing your race or class (not sure the order) and then you pick the other one. From there, you roll the dice for your skill points and you assign them as you go. We could have tooltips that suggest what categories the skillpoints should go into based on the class you picked. After you assign your skillpoints, it would automatically fill in your modifiers and all of the other stats that are derived from them. Then you would have a final chance to look everything over before you submit. Once you submit, then your characters skillpoints are final. Then you go through a menu where you choose your abilities/talents/spells, which are populated based on your class/race. Finally, you either accept 70g to start with or you roll the dice. Then you have the option to create a character backstory or finish!

Throughout the process, it will let you know what bonuses you get for certain classes or what extra skills get assigned to you for your decisions.

You could then upload a picture of your character.

I like mint.com’s (under profile) profile completion menu. Its slick.

More ideas- at the end of the character creation, it could auto generate a character sheet for you to print out (if you want).

I could make this editable for any online RPG or tabletop game.
